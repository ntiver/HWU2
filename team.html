OUR TEAM

We bring difference, tenacity and action in the fight for accessible communications through captioning.

Jessica Sharp

President

Jess is a change maker and embodies continuous improvement, never quite satisfied with the status quo. She studied business management at the University of Queensland (UQ) and works as a public servant.

Jess was hooked after learning about socially-constructed disability barriers, shifting the perspective to designing environments and communications to include people with differences as equal. Jess balances listening fatigue by working with her hands. She believes in increasing access to communication through captioning to enable more people to engage and connect in whatever they choose.

Shirley Edwards

Secretary

Shirley has a long history working with and for people with hearing loss in Queensland. She was Secretary of the organisation of CICADA QLD for over 25 years and has achieved many awards for her advocacy, voluntary and work on a variety of hearing loss issues, particularly with the captioned telephone when it was removed from the National Relay Service by the Federal Government.

Shirley, who has two cochlear implants, worked with AccessComm to promote the case of the captioning telephone to be returned to the NRS and why it is necessary for people living alone, especially the elderly and those who need the phone for their work, to have communication with the outside world. She successfully advocated for the use of Captel in the Redland Public Hospital, which is the only hospital known to offer this service to people with hearing loss in Queensland. Shirley was the second person in Australia to get the CapTel telephone in 2010. She is also the co-author of three books relating to cochlear implants.

Andrew Sharp

Treasurer

Andrew is a founding member of Hear With Us and an accomplished advocate for the hearing impaired. He is both a father and son of hearing impaired family members. He was educated at the University of Queensland as a professional engineer followed by an international corporate management career. He founded successful private companies in Australia and retired in 2012.

His involvement with the needs of the hearing impaired began nearly 40 years ago with the birth of his daughter and her journey through higher education and the workplace transition. Following years of encouragement (follow your dreams), the barriers to career fulfillment in the workplace provoked the bear. His interest in advocacy germinated in 2020 with the collapse of the National Relay Service captioned relay communication service. The explosion of screen technology cemented his belief in the importance of captions as an aid to communication.

Andrew has broadened his advocacy interests beyond the National Relay Service and campaigns for captioning as an aid to everyday communication, in public transport, tourism and within Government agencies.
